Money is in politics. Bigly.

In the 2016 election, nearly $6.6 billion was raised and spent by candidates campaigning for Federal office. We start by asking: Does raising more money than your opponent increase your chances of winning an election?

Data released for the 2016 election by the Federal Election Commission show that in a shocking 94% of races, the candidate who raised the most money also won the election. The effects of outraising your opponent are shown below by varying degrees:

So we built Kingmakers.

Kingmakers is a tool which guides you through the campaign finances of your elected representatives. This data, which the Federal Election Commission (FEC) makes publicly available, shows which individuals, PACs, and industries contribute to which campaigns, and how much is being contributed. There are many aspects to this data, so we've created several visualizations to help you find your way around.

As an example, let's look at a few of the visualization products and how they fit in with one another.
