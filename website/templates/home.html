Money is in politics. Bigly.

In the 2016 election, nearly $6.6 billion was raised and spent by candidates campaigning for Federal office. We start by asking: Does raising more money than your opponent increase your chances of winning an election? Data released for the 2016 election by the Federal Election Commission show that in a shocking 94% of races, the candidate who raised the most money also won the election.

The effects of outraising your opponent (shown above) indicate that as more money is raised relative to opponents, winning an election becomes a near certainty. Since money is such a powerful force in the political process, you can bet that politicians pay very close attention to their donors. We believe that it is important that constituents understand who contributes to the campaigns of their elected representatives so that they are aware of those whose voices carry the most weight once a candidate becomes a legislator.

So we built Kingmakers.

Kingmakers is a set of tools which guide you through the campaign finances of your elected representatives. This data, which the Federal Election Commission (FEC) makes publicly available, shows which individuals, PACs, and industries contribute to which campaigns, and how much is being contributed. We also include data from other organizations in this area, including OpenSecrets, ProPublica, and various contributors on GitHub.

With so much data coming from so many different sources, it can be difficult to orient yourself in the realm of political fundraising. Whether you're a curious first-time voter or a seasoned ballot slinger, you can use the Kingmakers tool to find your way around the campaign funds of your elected representative.

Let's look at how the Kingmakers tool can be used to understand the fundraising behind a political campaign.

Industry diagram

A profile of industry spending in politics. The top 100 contributions and their recipients are shown through linkages which show the funding priorities of different industries. Use this tool to generate ideas regarding what parties receive money from different industry groups. Within the diagram, click on a link to view the specific politicians receiving money from that particular industry.

Network visualization

Explore the interconnections between industry donations and politicians in a guided tour of campaign contributions. We highlight a few interesting cases and allow you explore the data yourself using guided drill-downs. (You might need a larger screen to get the full effect!)

Candidate dashboard

Dig even deeper into the funding of an individual candidate in order to see detailed campaign finance data. You can use this tool to see the specific informations on campaigns that you've already uncovered or to see data on a politician you are curious about.
